# Story Architecture Blueprint
### Based on Matthew Dicks' *Storyworthy*

> A story is the shortest distance between a person and a change.
> Move away from reporting events. Document transformation.

---

## 1. The Five-Second Moment (The "Why")

*The most important part. This is the moment where you realized something, changed your mind, or became a different person. If you don't have this, you have an anecdote — not a story.*

*The "Elephant" is the clear, central conflict introduced in the first minute. The stakes explain why we should care if you succeed or fail.*

## 3. The Beginning (The Opposite of the End)

*A story is a bridge. If your ending is a moment of courage, your beginning must show your cowardice. You want the greatest possible distance between your Start State and End State.*

## 5. The "Backpack" & "Breadcrumbs"

*The "Backpack" is when you share your inner thoughts and fears so the audience carries the weight with you. "Breadcrumbs" are hints you drop early so the ending feels earned, not random.*

*Once the Five-Second Moment happens, the story is over. Don't linger. Don't summarise the moral. Just land the plane.*

Look for the smallest, most mundane moments. You don't need a plane crash to have a story — you just need a moment where you saw the world slightly differently than you did five minutes prior.
