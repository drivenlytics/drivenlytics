## Content Type Definitions

**Educational tip** — A single, actionable insight about hang therapy, spinal decompression, or the damage of sitting. Grounded in science. Warm, authoritative tone.

**Myth-busting** — Challenges a common misconception ("You need to go to a chiropractor for this" / "You need to be strong to hang"). Outlaw 10% energy. Ends with the truth.

**Science fact** — A specific, cited or citable clinical finding. Short, credible, shareable. Good for LinkedIn and Pinterest.

**Explainer** — Explains a mechanism or concept (why hanging works, what spinal decompression actually does). Educational depth. Good for carousels.

**Testimonial/result** — Real or representative result. "After 2 weeks of hanging daily, my shoulder pain went from 8/10 to 2/10." Human, specific, believable.

**Transformation story** — Slightly longer narrative arc. Before state → discovery → after state. Emotional.

**Problem hook** — Opens on the pain. "Do you wake up with a stiff lower back every morning?" Outlaw/Caretaker blend.

**Villain naming** — Names sitting as the enemy directly. Provocative. "Sitting is destroying your spine. It's doing it right now."

**Solution reveal** — The pivot from problem to answer. "Here's what actually fixes it." Leads to the hang protocol.

**Relatable scenario** — A moment the avatar recognises. "You get up from your desk after 3 hours and your back seizes up." Human, empathetic.

**Quiz CTA** — Direct invitation to take the Spine Age Quiz or Shoulder Age Test. Benefit-led.

**Lead magnet** — Promotes the free guide or protocol PDF. Value-first.

**Final CTA** — Clearest call to action of the campaign. Visit hangtherapy.com. Take the quiz. Download the guide.
