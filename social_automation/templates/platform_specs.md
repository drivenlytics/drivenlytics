# Platform Specs — HangTherapy Social Media
*Reference for caption_writer agent. These rules are non-negotiable per platform.*

---

## Instagram

- **Character limit:** 2,200 (optimal: 150–300 for feed, full 2,200 for carousel)
- **Image ratio:** 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (portrait) — portrait gets more feed real estate
- **Tone:** Inspirational, warm, visual-first. Lead with a hook in the first line (that's all people see before "more")
- **Hashtags:** 5–15, placed at end or in first comment. Mix broad (#shoulderpain) and niche (#hangtherapy #spinaldecompression)
- **CTA style:** Soft — "Save this", "Tag someone who needs this", "Link in bio"
- **Best for:** Educational carousels, transformation visuals, lifestyle content, short tips
- **Hook pattern:** Bold statement or question in line 1. Never start with "I" or the brand name.

**Example opening hooks:**
- "Your shoulders aren't aging. They're compressing."
- "10 seconds a day. That's it."
- "Nobody told you sitting was doing this to your spine."

---

## TikTok

- **Character limit:** 300 (caption), 150 optimal
- **Image ratio:** 9:16 (vertical) — full screen
- **Tone:** Hook-first, casual, fast. First 3 words must stop the scroll.
- **Hashtags:** 3–5, highly relevant only. TikTok's algorithm is topic-based, not hashtag-based.
- **CTA style:** Direct — "Follow for more", "Comment if this is you", "Link in bio for the free guide"
- **Best for:** Problem/solution content, quick tips, myth-busting, relatable moments
- **Hook pattern:** Start with the payoff or the problem. "I fixed my shoulder pain in 30 seconds." "Why your back hurts every morning."

**Example opening hooks:**
- "This fixed my shoulder pain in 30 seconds →"
- "Nobody talks about this sitting damage"
- "The 30-second hang that changes everything"

---

## Facebook

- **Character limit:** 500 optimal (platform allows more but engagement drops)
- **Image ratio:** 1.91:1 (landscape) for link posts, 1:1 for standalone images
- **Tone:** Conversational, community-oriented, slightly longer form than other platforms
- **Hashtags:** 1–3 max, or none. Facebook hashtags have minimal discovery impact.
- **CTA style:** Engagement-first — "Have you tried this?", "Drop a comment below", "Share with someone who needs this"
- **Best for:** Educational posts, social proof, relatable stories, community questions
- **Hook pattern:** Start with a question or relatable scenario. Facebook audiences respond to being spoken to directly.

**Example opening hooks:**
- "Have you ever woken up with a stiff back and wondered why?"
- "A quick question for anyone who sits for work:"
- "This is for the 40+ crowd dealing with shoulder pain silently."

---

## LinkedIn

- **Character limit:** 3,000 (optimal: 1,300 for regular posts, 3,000 for thought leadership)
- **Image ratio:** 1.91:1 (landscape) or 1:1
- **Tone:** Professional, authoritative, thought leadership. Personal story angles perform well.
- **Hashtags:** 3–5, professional and topic-based (#spinalhealth #longevity #workplacehealth)
- **CTA style:** Value-driven — "I wrote a full breakdown at HangTherapy.com", "Take the free Spine Age Quiz"
- **Best for:** Authority content, data-backed posts, professional health angles, biohacker audience
- **Hook pattern:** Bold claim or counterintuitive insight in line 1. LinkedIn rewards content that makes professionals stop and think.

**Example opening hooks:**
- "Sitting is costing companies billions in lost productivity. Here's what nobody's addressing."
- "I've spent 10 years studying spinal compression. Here's what I found."
- "The longevity protocol that takes 30 seconds and most people have never heard of."

## Threads

- **Character limit:** 500
- **Image ratio:** 1:1 or 4:5
- **Tone:** Casual, direct, conversational. Like a smart friend texting you a tip.
- **Hashtags:** Optional, 0–3. Threads discovery is still evolving.
- **CTA style:** Conversational — "Thoughts?", "Anyone else notice this?", "Reply if you want the full breakdown"
- **Best for:** Quick takes, opinions, relatable observations, casual tips
- **Hook pattern:** Short and direct. No preamble.

**Example opening hooks:**
- "Hanging for 30 seconds decompresses what hours of sitting compresses."
- "Your spine is aging faster than you are. Not a threat — a fixable fact."
- "The simplest health protocol most people have never tried."

## Bluesky

- **Character limit:** 300
- **Image ratio:** 1.91:1 (landscape) preferred
- **Tone:** Informational, clean, link-card friendly. Bluesky audience skews tech/health-literate.
- **Hashtags:** None (Bluesky uses topic feeds, not hashtags)
- **CTA style:** Link-forward — "Full breakdown:" or "Read more:"
- **Best for:** Clean informational posts, linking to blog content, short science-backed claims
- **Hook pattern:** State the fact or finding directly. Then link.

**Example opening hooks:**
- "98% of shoulder pain is treatable without surgery. Most people don't know this."
- "Hanging for 30 seconds triggers spinal decompression. Here's the mechanism:"
- "The average desk worker compresses their spine for 8+ hours daily."

## X (Twitter)

- **Character limit:** 280
- **Image ratio:** 16:9 (landscape)
- **Tone:** Punchy, sharp, opinionated. X rewards hot takes and clear positions.
- **Hashtags:** 1–2 max. Hashtags in the text look better than bolted on at the end.
- **CTA style:** Bold — "HangTherapy.com", "Thread below 🧵", "RT if you needed to hear this"
- **Best for:** Myth-busting, outlaw content, sharp stats, bold claims, controversy-adjacent health truths
- **Hook pattern:** The most provocative version of the truth. Short sentences. No wasted words.

**Example opening hooks:**
- "Sitting is the new smoking. And nobody is doing anything about it."
- "Surgery for shoulder pain has a 40% failure rate. Hanging has a 98% success rate."
- "You don't have bad posture. You have a compression problem nobody told you how to fix."

## Pinterest

- **Character limit:** 500 (description), 100 (title)
- **Image ratio:** 2:3 (vertical/portrait) — this is the Pinterest standard
- **Tone:** Keyword-rich, searchable, evergreen. Pinterest is a search engine, not a social feed.
- **Hashtags:** 2–5, keyword-focused
- **CTA style:** Direct benefit — "Learn how", "Free guide at HangTherapy.com", "Save for later"
- **Best for:** Educational content, guides, tips, lead magnet promotion, evergreen health content
- **Hook pattern:** Lead with the benefit or keyword. Pinterest users are searching, not scrolling.

**Example titles:**
- "How to Relieve Shoulder Pain Naturally in 30 Seconds"
- "Spinal Decompression at Home — The Hanging Protocol"
- "Fix Back Pain from Sitting: The Hang Therapy Guide"

---

## YouTube Shorts

- **Character limit:** 100 (title), 5,000 (description but rarely read)
- **Image ratio:** 9:16 (vertical) — full screen
- **Tone:** Hook-focused, educational, punchy. First 3 seconds must hook.
- **Hashtags:** 3–5 in description (#shorts required, plus topic tags)
- **CTA style:** Subscribe-focused or link in bio
- **Best for:** Repurposed long-form content, explainers, transformation stories, quick protocols
- **Hook pattern:** Title IS the hook. Treat it like a headline, not a description.

**Example titles:**
- "Fix Shoulder Pain in 30 Seconds #shorts"
- "This Is What Sitting Does to Your Spine #hangtherapy"
- "The Protocol That Decompresses Your Spine #shorts"

## Brand Voice Reminders (apply to all platforms)

- **Therapeutic language, not fitness language** — "protocol" not "workout", "decompression" not "exercise"
- **Customer is the hero** — they are solving their problem; HangTherapy is just the guide
- **80% warm/caring, 10% simple/natural, 10% disruptive/outlaw**
- **Never:** hypey claims, fitness-bro tone, fear without hope, medical jargon without explanation
- **Always:** acknowledge the pain first, then offer the path forward
- **Science is credibility** — reference clinical backing where possible
