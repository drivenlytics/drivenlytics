# Thai RE Startup — Pitch Talking Points
**Client:** Friend / startup (Singaporean + Japanese foreign property buyers in Thailand)
**Situation:** Friend has been a real estate agent for foreigners in Thailand. Now partnered with a big investor to launch a startup. His ask: "build me a website."

---

## 1. Acknowledge the ask — then reframe

> "You want a website. Totally get it. But a website alone won't do what you actually need.
> What you need is a machine that generates qualified buyer leads from Singapore and Japan, AND pulls in Thai sellers who want to reach foreign buyers — and converts both sides before your competitors even know they exist.
> The website is the front door. What I build is everything behind it."

---

## 2. Frame the market moment — timing is the edge

- Thailand just changed the law. Foreign ownership is now legally accessible in ways it wasn't before.
- This window is open right now — and it's temporary.
- The agents who build digital trust with Singaporean and Japanese investors in the next 6 months will be the ones those investors call for the next 10 years.
- Right now: there's almost no competition for the digital space targeting this exact buyer. Low cost of attention. High receptivity.
- When other agencies catch up, the cost to acquire that same attention goes up 5–10x.

> "We build your presence now. You own the category before anyone else is even thinking about it."

---

## 3. Dollar-value the opportunity

### The numbers (conservative)
- Bangkok condo sweet spot for Singaporean/Japanese investors: **8M–12M THB (~$220K–$330K USD)**
- Typical commission structure in Thailand: 3–5% (seller pays); buyer-side 1–3%
- Blended average: 10M THB deal at 3% = **~$8,000 per closing**
- 3 closings/month = **~$24,000/month gross commission**
- 5 closings/month = **~$40,000/month gross commission**
- Marketing investment to get there: $3K–$5K/month

> "That's $6–$8 back for every $1 spent on marketing. Show me another investment with that return."

### The two revenue streams most agents miss
Most agents chase only one side. There are two:
- **Buyer side** — foreigners searching for Thai investment property
- **Seller side** — Thai property owners who want foreign buyers

Competing on both sides = higher deal flow + higher commission per deal + a defensible position in the market.

---

## 4. The two-sided system

### Buyer Lead Machine
Target: Singaporeans and Japanese investors researching Thai property

- SEO content: "foreigner buy condo Bangkok", "Thai property investment Singapore", "new Thailand foreign ownership law"
- Social targeting: Facebook, Instagram, Line (massive in Thailand/Japan), YouTube
- Education content on the new law — captures early-stage researchers before they find a competitor
- Remarketing funnel → email nurture sequence → consultation booking
- Content builds trust before they ever pick up the phone

### Seller Lead Machine
Target: Thai property owners who want to attract foreign buyers

- Listing presentation materials — market reports, buyer ICA profiles, case studies
- Market report content that positions your startup as the authority on foreign buyer demand
- SWOT analysis, seller checklist, and listing assets — a complete system
- These are proven frameworks from the US market, adapted for the Thai context

> "Most agencies can do one of these things. Nobody's built the full system for this specific market yet. That's the opportunity."

## 6. On "innovation" — sell the outcome, not the concept

Don't sell innovation. Sell what innovation produces.

- "No one has built this system for the Thai-Singapore property market yet. We build it first. You own the category."
- "Your competitors are all doing the same thing — that's why they all get the same results. We do something different. Different approach, different position, different results."
- "Anyone can build a website. What we're building is a market position. That's the asset."
- "Digital trust compounds. The authority you build in year one pays dividends in year three. The agents who wait until it's obvious will be playing catch-up forever."
