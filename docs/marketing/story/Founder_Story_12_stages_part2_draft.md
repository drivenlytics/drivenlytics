# The Founder — Part 2 (Draft)

> [One-line hook]

---

## 1. Ordinary World

Who were you before all this? Set the scene. Where you came from,
what life looked like, what you were doing.

Every morning I put on a suit and walked into the stock tower. I was selling mutual funds wholesale to the third-party broker dealer channel — wholesale banking. Corporate. Structured. A scoreboard with a number on it and a suit to match.

I was competitive. I was good at it. But the setting — the world around me — wasn't built for someone like me.

---

## 2. Call to Adventure

What disrupted the ordinary? The moment, the problem, the opportunity
that showed up and changed everything.

I did the job. I won. But there was no meaning to it. No value at the end of the day. Just a number on a scoreboard.

Then the team lead who hired me was switched out. The new one was underperforming and placing the blame on me. Everyone knew he couldn't sell. And I knew I was done.

I decided to pursue my entrepreneurial desires — completely left field — in online marketing. This was the cost of Gen X crossing into the digital age. I knew nothing about it. But I had ideas that were validated by unicorn businesses. I had vision. I had creativity. I lacked the skills.

I went ahead anyway.

---

## 3. Refusal of the Call

What made you hesitate? The doubt, the risk, the reasons not to do it.
Be honest here — it makes the rest land harder.

Like most people who weren't brought up in the technical age, there was a large amount of hesitancy. The lack of knowledge. The lack of security. It was a big change and I knew it wouldn't be easy.

That fear was real. I'm not going to pretend it wasn't.

---

## 4. Meeting the Mentor

Who or what gave you the conviction to move? A person, an experience,
a realization. What shifted your thinking.

I knew leadership would determine my ceiling of success. I didn't want a ceiling. So I invested in myself — took a leadership course and started down that path.

Then I took my natural strengths — a people person who excelled in sales — and applied them to the area of marketing that would deliver the most success. I niched down. I delivered.

Within three months I was working for some of the biggest marketers in the world. People making millions of dollars. After that, significant job offers and opportunities followed.

---

## 5. Crossing the Threshold

The point of no return. The decision you made and what you left behind.

The whole point of getting into online marketing was so I could start a business. Build something for myself. Pursue and fulfil my dreams.

I was willing to take the long route to build the foundation for long-term success.

That was eight years ago.

---

## 6. Tests, Allies & Enemies

What you ran into once you committed. The hard lessons, the people who
helped, the obstacles that almost broke it.

As I exited the freelance and consulting world I'd worked so hard to build, I knew I needed to empty the cup. I didn't want tinted glasses forcing me toward quick wins or early success. I wanted a journey that would deliver long-term happiness.

So I aligned my passion for health and wellness with hardware — to bring real value to a market at scale.

And when you do something that hard, you find out who's with you. You build allies. You encounter enemies. Without a doubt.

---

## 7. Approach

How you adapted. The strategy that emerged. What you learned to do
differently as you got deeper in.

My approach was hardened by my work ethic.

Before leaving the freelance world, I was doing workouts that most people wouldn't attempt. Every second day: 800 jumping jacks as a warm-up. Then 200 jumping jack burpees — 20 jumping jacks, 10 push-ups, no break, under 9 minutes. Then 300 lunges. Then core work. Then strength training. Sometimes daily. I did this working 8 PM to 5 AM.

I hated those days. I did them anyway.

Because in business, everything goes wrong. Almost all the time. It always wants to pull you off course, throw problems at you, create chaos. You have to persist day in and day out. You have to have mental toughness.

I understand that. And I understand you.

---

## 8. Ordeal, Death & Rebirth

The darkest moment. What nearly ended it. What you had to let die
so something better could take its place.

I've worked with people who lied to me. Who wasted my time. Who bullshitted me and made excuses.

It was heartbreaking.

I had to make the tough calls. Move on. Maintain the relationship where possible. But keep going toward the goal regardless.

That process of letting go — of people, of paths, of expectations — is what made room for what came next.

---

## 9. Reward, Seizing the Sword

What you won. The insight, the capability, the proof. What you came back with that you didn't have before.

Eventually the reward came — not in a contract or a number, but in the relationships I'd built. Supportive entrepreneurs who forged alongside me, helped me build an early-stage prototype, and helped me get to where I am today.

I couldn't have done that without them.

You need these people on your journey. I know how hard it is to find them.

I get you.

---

## 10. The Road Back

The decision to bring it back. The commitment to apply everything learned in service of something bigger.

It was at that point I realized what I'd accumulated.

Over eight years I'd built a unique skill set: the ability to take data, sales psychology, and brand — combine them into individual imagery and sales messaging that increases conversions. Words that sell. Stories that connect. Systems that deliver.

I'd applied this for myself, for clients, and I knew it could help thousands of entrepreneurs who don't have access to this kind of thinking.

So I came full circle.

---

## 11. Resurrection

The final test. The moment where everything — every lesson, every scar — got applied. What came out the other side.

---

There will always be another road to walk.

And at some point, NO ONE WILL BELIEVE YOU. No one will want to help. People will doubt you. 100%. Because they don't understand — and they likely never will — because you're built different.

The moment you bet on yourself is when you win. It's always been the case.

What you now give. The transformation clients experience working with you. This is the promise — stated plainly, no hype.

---

I understand how difficult it is. I've lived it. And I've been built — skill by skill, scar by scar — to deliver something that very few people can.

Where my unique skills overlap is where breakthroughs happen.

That's what I bring to your business.
