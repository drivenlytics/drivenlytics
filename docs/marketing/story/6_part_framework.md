## 2. The Stakes & The "Elephant"

- **What is the "Elephant" (the obvious goal or problem)?**
  > A guy in a corporate suit who was born to build things — trying to find his way out and into something that meant something.

- **What is at risk if you fail? (Emotional or physical stakes)**
  > Staying on the scoreboard forever. Winning at something that doesn't matter. Becoming the version of yourself your parents expected, not the one you knew you were.

---

## 3. The Beginning (The Opposite of the End)

- **How were you the opposite of your Five-Second Moment at the start?**
  > At the start: following the herd, wearing the suit, winning on someone else's terms. At the end: building on your own terms, eight years in, in full possession of your skills and your direction.

- **The First Sentence:** *(Start as close to the end as possible. No "Once upon a time.")*
  > [ e.g. "Every morning I put on a suit, walked into the stock tower, and told myself this was winning." ]

---

## 4. The Narrative Arc (But & Therefore)

- **Inciting Incident:**
  > I was good at the job. Competitive. Winning. But there was no meaning to it — just a number on a scoreboard.

- **But... (The obstacle):**
  > The team lead who hired me was switched out. The new one couldn't sell — and placed the blame on me. Everyone knew it. I was done.

- **Therefore... (Your reaction):**
  > I decided to pursue something completely left field — online marketing. Gen X crossing into the digital age. I knew nothing about it. I went ahead anyway.

- **But... (The escalation):**
  > The skills weren't there. The security wasn't there. The knowledge wasn't there. People lied to me, wasted my time, let me down. The hardware startup tested everything I had.

- **Therefore... (The final push toward the climax):**
  > I kept going. Hardened by the work ethic. Persistence over everything. And eight years later, I came out the other side holding something rare — a skill set almost nobody else has.

## 6. The Resolution (The Landing)

- **The Final Sentence:** *(Connect back to the beginning or the change.)*
  > [ e.g. "I don't wear a suit anymore. But I still only know one mode — work. The difference is, now it's mine." ]

## Notes & Alternatives

- The Five-Second Moment is not yet locked in — options to consider:
  1. The moment you decided to leave the corporate job
  2. The moment the bad team lead appeared and you realised the system wasn't going to reward you
  3. The moment during the hardware startup you realised you'd built something rare
  4. The moment you decided to come full circle and apply everything to help other entrepreneurs

- The strongest candidate is probably **option 3 or 4** — the moment of realising the accumulation of skills was the point, not the specific projects.
