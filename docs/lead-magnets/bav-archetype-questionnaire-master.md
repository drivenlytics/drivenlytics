# BAV Archetype Questionnaire

**Purpose:** To identify your brand's core motivation and Carl Jung archetype using the BAV (BrandAsset Valuator) 48 attributes framework.

---

## Part 1: Core Motivations

Brands anchor in one of four core human motivations. Which of these best describes what drives your brand's relationship with its customers?

**1. What is the fundamental human need your brand fulfills for your customers? (Select your top choice)**

- [ ] **Yearning for Paradise (Independence & Fulfillment):** We help people find happiness, discover the world, or seek truth. *(Examples: Starbucks, The New York Times, Coca-Cola)*
- [ ] **Leaving a Thumbprint (Mastery & Risk):** We help people prove their worth, spark a revolution, or turn visions into reality. *(Examples: Nike, Apple, Disney)*
- [ ] **No Man is an Island (Belonging & Enjoyment):** We help people connect with others, experience intimacy, or simply have a great time. *(Examples: Wendy's, Hallmark, Pepsi)*
- [ ] **Providing Structure (Stability & Control):** We help people protect others, create enduring value, or establish successful order. *(Examples: Johnson & Johnson, IBM, Crayola)*

## Part 2: Imagery Attributes

Review the following Brand Imagery Attributes. Select the **top 5 to 7 attributes** that you feel *most strongly* and *authentically* represent your brand's personality today.

*(Contextual examples have been provided to help guide your selections.)*

### Strength & Leadership
- [ ] **Leader:** Commanding and guiding. *(E.g., You set the industry standard, like Microsoft.)*
- [ ] **Strong:** Powerful and highly competent. *(E.g., You overcome difficult challenges, like FedEx.)*
- [ ] **Reliable:** Dependable, consistent, and safe. *(E.g., You provide stability, like Volvo.)*
- [ ] **Capable:** Having the fitness or quality necessary to achieve results.

### Innovation & Vision
- [ ] **Innovative:** Ahead of the curve, introducing new ideas. *(E.g., Giving form to a vision, like Apple/iMac).*
- [ ] **Visionary:** Thinking about the future with imagination. *(E.g., Making dreams come true, like Disney).*
- [ ] **Original:** Unique and not derived from others.
- [ ] **Creative:** Involving imagination or original ideas.

### Connection & Empathy
- [ ] **Caring:** Displaying kindness and concern for others. *(E.g., Nurturing and protecting, like Campbell's Soup).*
- [ ] **Warm:** Showing enthusiasm, affection, or kindness.
- [ ] **Helpful:** Giving or ready to give help.
- [ ] **Friendly:** Pleasant and approachable.

### Authenticity & Truth
- [ ] **Authentic:** Genuine and true to its origins. *(E.g., Seeking absolute truth, like Harvard).*
- [ ] **Genuine:** Truly what something is said to be. *(E.g., Realism and lack of pretense, like Levi's).*
- [ ] **Down-to-earth:** Practical, realistic, with no illusions.
- [ ] **Trustworthy:** Able to be relied on as honest or truthful.

### Disruption & Boldness
- [ ] **Daring:** Adventurous or audaciously bold. *(E.g., Breaking boundaries, like Patagonia or Harley-Davidson).*
- [ ] **Outspoken:** Frank in stating opinions, even controversial ones.
- [ ] **Different:** Unlike others in nature, form, or quality.
- [ ] **Distinctive:** Standing out from the crowd.

### Passion & Enjoyment
- [ ] **Fun:** Providing enjoyment and amusement. *(E.g., Living in the moment, like M&Ms).*
- [ ] **Social:** Seeking or enjoying the companionship of others.
- [ ] **Cool:** Fashionable and attractive.
- [ ] **Sophisticated:** Revealing worldly experience. *(E.g., Sensuous and elegant, like Chanel).*
