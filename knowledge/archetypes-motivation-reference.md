# Archetype & Motivation Reference
**Source:** The Hero and The Outlaw — Margaret Mark & Carol S. Pearson  
**Purpose:** Brand strategy reference for Drivenlytics

---

## Motivation Framework

Brands anchor in one of four core human motivations sitting along two axes:

**Axis 1:** Belonging/Enjoyment ↔ Independence/Self-Actualization  
**Axis 2:** Stability/Control ↔ Risk/Mastery

| Quadrant | Motivation | Archetypes |
|---|---|---|
| **Yearning for Paradise** | Independence & Fulfillment | Innocent, Explorer, Sage |
| **Leaving a Thumbprint on the World** | Mastery & Risk | Hero, Outlaw, Magician |
| **No Man is an Island** | Belonging & Enjoyment | Regular Guy/Gal, Lover, Jester |
| **Providing Structure to the World** | Stability & Control | Caregiver, Creator, Ruler |

---

## The 12 Archetypes

---

### The Innocent
- **Core Desire:** To experience paradise
- **Goal:** To be happy
- **Fear:** Doing something wrong that will provoke punishment
- **Strategy:** Do things right
- **Gift:** Faith and optimism
- **Shadow:** Denial, repression

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Childlike simplicity, naive, dependent, obedient, trusting, idyllic
- **Level 2:** Renewal, positive, reinventing, reframing, cleansing, reentering the Promised Land
- **Level 3:** Almost mystical oneness — innocence comes from values and integrity, not outer experience; being, not doing

**Brand Examples:** Ivory Soap, Coca-Cola, McDonald's, Breyers, Disney, Keds  
**Brand Signals:** Nostalgic, simple, wholesome. Pastel colors, uncluttered layouts, natural elements. Messaging promises life can be uncomplicated, predictable, and good.

---

### The Explorer
- **Core Desire:** Freedom to find out who you are through exploring the world
- **Goal:** To experience a better, more authentic, more fulfilling life
- **Fear:** Getting trapped, conforming, inner emptiness
- **Strategy:** Journey, seek out new things, escape from entrapment and boredom
- **Gift:** Autonomy, ambition, ability to be true to one's own soul
- **Shadow:** So alienated you cannot find any way to fit in

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Hitting the open road, going into nature, exploring the world
- **Level 2:** Seeking your own individuality, to individuate, to become fulfilled
- **Level 3:** Expressing individuality and uniqueness

**Brand Examples:** Starbucks, Levi's, Amazon, Ford Explorer, Jeep Wrangler, Patagonia  
**Brand Signals:** Rugged, sturdy, open skies, open road, nature. Values independence, "no boundaries," nonconformity, authenticity, and discovery over hype.

---

### The Sage
- **Core Desire:** The discovery of truth
- **Goal:** To use intelligence and analysis to understand the world
- **Fear:** Being duped, misled; ignorance
- **Strategy:** Seek out information and knowledge; become self-reflective
- **Gift:** Wisdom, intelligence
- **Shadow:** Dogmatism, ivory tower, disconnection from reality

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Search for absolute truth, desire for objectivity, looking to experts
- **Level 2:** Skepticism, critical and innovative thinking, becoming an expert
- **Level 3:** Wisdom, confidence, mastery

**Brand Examples:** Harvard, Mayo Clinic, McKinsey, The New York Times, CNN, Barnes & Noble  
**Brand Signals:** Dignified, subdued aesthetics (gray, beige, white, light blue). Hard data, research, expert recommendation. Treats the consumer as intelligent and capable of rational choice.

---

### The Hero
- **Core Desire:** Prove worth through courageous and difficult action
- **Goal:** Exert mastery in a way that improves the world
- **Fear:** Weakness, vulnerability, "wimping out"
- **Strategy:** Become as strong, competent, and powerful as possible
- **Gift:** Competence and courage
- **Shadow:** Ruthlessness and obsessive need to win

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Development of boundaries, competence, mastery through achievement and competition
- **Level 2:** Doing your duty for your country, organization, community, or family
- **Level 3:** Using strength and courage for something that makes a difference to you and the world

**Brand Examples:** Nike, FedEx, U.S. Army, Red Cross, the Olympics, March of Dimes  
**Brand Signals:** Energetic, dramatic, purposeful, intense. Strong colors, definitive lines. Functional, challenging environments. Avoids comfort that might make one "soft."

---

### The Outlaw
- **Core Desire:** Revenge or revolution
- **Goal:** To destroy what is not working
- **Fear:** Being powerless, trivialized, inconsequential
- **Strategy:** Disrupt, destroy, or shock
- **Gift:** Outrageousness, radical freedom
- **Shadow:** Criminal or evil behavior

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Identifying as an outsider, dissociating from conventional values and morality
- **Level 2:** Behaving in shocking or disruptive ways
- **Level 3:** Becoming a rebel or a revolutionary

**Brand Examples:** Harley-Davidson, Apple (1984 campaign), MTV, Howard Stern, Calvin Klein, Virgin  
**Brand Signals:** Edgy, shocking, provocative. Dark, shadowy qualities with intense colors. Appeals to alienation, rule-breaking, and the thrill of forbidden fruit. Marketed through alternative channels.

---

### The Magician
- **Core Desire:** Knowledge of the fundamental laws of how the world works
- **Goal:** Make dreams come true
- **Fear:** Unanticipated negative consequences
- **Strategy:** Develop vision and live it
- **Gift:** Finding win-win outcomes
- **Shadow:** Manipulation, sorcery

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Magical moments and experiences of transformation
- **Level 2:** The experience of flow
- **Level 3:** Miracles — moving from vision to manifestation

**Brand Examples:** MasterCard ("Priceless"), Disney, Calgon, Smirnoff, Lucent Technologies  
**Brand Signals:** Exotic, ancient, or ritualistic imagery (caves, stars, celestial events). Shimmering, translucent, changeable qualities. Messaging focuses on consciousness, transformation, and turning visions into reality.

---

### The Regular Guy/Gal
- **Core Desire:** Connection with others
- **Goal:** To belong, fit in
- **Fear:** Standing out, seeming to put on airs, being rejected
- **Strategy:** Develop ordinary solid virtues, the common touch, blend in
- **Gift:** Realism, empathy, lack of pretense
- **Shadow:** The victim willing to be abused rather than be alone

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** The orphan — feeling abandoned and alone, seeking affiliation
- **Level 2:** The joiner — learning to connect, fit in, accept help and friendship
- **Level 3:** The humanitarian — believing in the natural dignity of every person regardless of abilities or circumstances

**Brand Examples:** Wendy's, Saturn, Levi's, Visa, J.C. Penney  
**Brand Signals:** Down-home, folksy, authentic, unpretentious. Self-deprecating humor. The "third place" where everyone is equal. Eschews hype, elitism, and artifice.

---

### The Lover
- **Core Desire:** Attain intimacy and experience sensual pleasure
- **Goal:** Being in a relationship with the people, work, and experiences they love
- **Fear:** Being alone, unwanted, unloved
- **Strategy:** Become more and more attractive — physically, emotionally, in every way
- **Gift:** Passion, gratitude, appreciation, commitment
- **Shadow:** Promiscuity, obsession, jealousy, envy

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Seeking great sex or great romance
- **Level 2:** Following your bliss and committing to whom and what you love
- **Level 3:** Spiritual love, self-acceptance, and the experience of ecstasy

**Brand Examples:** Hallmark, Victoria's Secret, Revlon, Godiva, Barilla, Jaguar, Chanel  
**Brand Signals:** Sensuous, elegant, aesthetic, indulgent. Highly tailored services that make the customer feel special. Messaging focuses on beauty, romance, intimacy, passion, and transformation.

---

### The Jester
- **Core Desire:** To live in the moment with full enjoyment
- **Goal:** To have a great time and lighten up the world
- **Fear:** Boredom or being boring
- **Strategy:** Play, make jokes, be funny
- **Gift:** Joy
- **Shadow:** Self-indulgence, irresponsibility, mean-spirited pranks

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Life as a game, fun
- **Level 2:** Cleverness used to trick others, get out of trouble, find ways around obstacles
- **Level 3:** Life experienced in the moment, one day at a time

**Brand Examples:** Miller Lite, Pepsi, Ben & Jerry's, M&Ms, Snickers  
**Brand Signals:** Bright colors, high energy, zany, playful, irreverent. Uses humor to deflate pomposity. Anarchistic, rule-breaking (for fun, not revolution), and spontaneous.

---

### The Caregiver
- **Core Desire:** Protect people from harm
- **Goal:** To help others
- **Fear:** Selfishness, ingratitude
- **Strategy:** Do things for others
- **Gift:** Compassion, generosity
- **Shadow:** Martyrdom, enabling, guilt-tripping

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Caring for and nurturing one's dependents
- **Level 2:** Balancing self-care with care for others
- **Level 3:** Altruism, concern for the larger world

**Brand Examples:** AT&T, Johnson & Johnson, Campbell's Soup, Volvo, Blue Cross/Blue Shield, Marriott  
**Brand Signals:** Soft, nurturing, reassuring imagery. Focuses on human vulnerability, safety, and family. Emphasizes superior service, empathy, consistency, and actions that prove the brand cares.

---

### The Creator
- **Core Desire:** Create something of enduring value
- **Goal:** Give form to a vision
- **Fear:** Having a mediocre vision or execution
- **Strategy:** Develop artistic control and skill
- **Gift:** Creativity and imagination
- **Shadow:** Overly dramatizing your life, living a soap opera

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Being creative or innovative in imitative ways
- **Level 2:** Giving form to your own vision
- **Level 3:** Creating structures that influence culture and society

**Brand Examples:** Crayola, Martha Stewart, Williams-Sonoma, Apple (iMac), Kinko's, Sesame Street  
**Brand Signals:** Nonconformist, expressive, highly aesthetic. Unique and iconoclastic designs. Demands high quality, shuns mass-produced merchandise. Marketing often engages the customer in co-creating the product.

---

### The Ruler
- **Core Desire:** Control
- **Goal:** Create a prosperous, successful family, company, or community
- **Fear:** Chaos, being overthrown
- **Strategy:** Exert leadership
- **Gift:** Responsibility, leadership
- **Shadow:** Tyrannical or manipulative behaviors

**Three Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Taking responsibility for the state of your own life
- **Level 2:** Exerting leadership in your family, group, organization, or workplace
- **Level 3:** Becoming a leader in your community, field, or society

**Brand Examples:** IBM, American Express, Brooks Brothers, Microsoft, Citibank, Ralph Lauren  
**Brand Signals:** Substantial, impressive, timeless aesthetics (columns, heavy materials, traditional cuts). Messaging focuses on status, prestige, and the power to make life stable. Respects hierarchy and sets industry standards.
